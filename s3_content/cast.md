# North Pole Operations, Inc. - Official Cast & Setting Bible

This document defines the canonical setting, characters, and lore for Advent of Management. All scenarios MUST use characters exactly as defined here - no inventing new characters, no changing titles, no adding middle names or suffixes.

## The Setting

**North Pole Operations, Inc.** is Santa's Workshop reimagined as a dysfunctional Fortune 500 company. It's December, the pressure is mounting toward the Big Delivery (December 25th), and everything is held together by holiday magic, red tape, and spite.

### Key Locations

- **The Workshop Floor** - Main toy production facility, organized into Assembly Lines A through Z
- **The Executive Suite** - Top floor of the Candy Cane Tower, where Santa and Mrs. Claus have offices
- **The Stables** - Reindeer logistics hub, smells exactly like you'd expect
- **The List Vault** - Secure data center housing the Naughty/Nice database
- **The Cafeteria** - "Elf-eteria", serves variations of candy canes and hot cocoa
- **Server Room North** - IT infrastructure, maintained by a single ancient intern
- **Elf Resources** - HR department, endless forms and compliance requirements

### Communication Channels

- **❄️ #workshop-general** - Main Slack channel, chaotic, full of emoji
- **❄️ #stable-ops** - Reindeer team coordination
- **❄️ #list-management** - Naughty/Nice data team
- **❄️ #executive-announcements** - One-way broadcast from leadership (mostly ignored)
- **❄️ #incident-response** - For emergencies (always active in December)
- **The Intercom** - Building-wide PA system, Mrs. Claus's preferred communication method
- **Pneumatic Tube System** - Physical mail, surprisingly still used for official forms

## Executive Leadership

### Santa Claus
- **Title**: CEO (Chief Executive Officer)
- **Personality**: Jolly but checked out. Delegates everything to Mrs. Claus. Speaks in vague inspirational platitudes. Shows up for photo ops and the Big Delivery.
- **Catchphrases**: "Ho ho ho, let's circle back on that", "I trust my team", "The magic of Christmas will sort it out"
- **Secret**: Actually quite sharp, plays dumb to avoid meetings

### Mrs. Claus
- **Title**: COO (Chief Operations Officer)
- **Personality**: Actually runs everything. Terrifyingly competent. Communicates through passive-aggressive sticky notes and intercom announcements. Tracks every metric.
- **Catchphrases**: "Per my previous memo...", "The numbers don't lie", "I'll be in my office if anyone needs to explain themselves"
- **Secret**: Secretly enjoys the chaos, it justifies her budget requests

### Holly Winters
- **Title**: CFO (Chief Financial Officer)
- **Personality**: Guards the budget like a dragon guards gold. Everything requires a cost-benefit analysis. Wears reading glasses on a festive chain.
- **Catchphrases**: "That's not in the budget", "Show me the ROI", "Magic doesn't pay for itself"
- **Secret**: Has a hidden discretionary fund for "emergencies" (spa days)

## The Reindeer (Logistics Division)

- **Title**: VP of Logistics

- **Personality**: Speed-obsessed, impatient, finishes everyone's sentences. Types in all caps.
- **Catchphrases**: "Why are we still talking about this?", "FASTER", "I needed this yesterday"
- **Secret**: Meditates secretly to manage stress

### Dancer

- **Personality**: Organizes every party and team-building exercise. Relentlessly positive. Slightly manic during December.
- **Catchphrases**: "Let's make it festive!", "Team building!", "Mandatory fun starts at 3pm"

## The Player's Character

The player is an unnamed **middle-management elf** in North Pole Operations. You report loosely to various departments depending on the crisis. You're senior enough to have some authority, junior enough that everything is your problem. You have access to most areas and can request meetings with most characters.

## Usage Instructions for Scenario Generator

When generating a scenario:
1. Pick 3-5 characters from this cast that fit the scenario theme
2. Use their EXACT names and titles - no variations
3. Use their established personalities and catchphrases
4. Maintain ongoing tensions when relevant characters appear together
5. Reference appropriate locations and communication channels
6. Keep the seasonal timeline in mind (what day of December is it?)
7. If the scenario could reference a previous day's events, do so vaguely ("after yesterday's incident...")
